The Y Combinator Era

Sam Altman's transformative leadership from 2014 to 2019

Leadership Role

Sam Altman served as the president of Y Combinator from 2014 to 2019, taking over from co-founder Paul Graham. His vision and leadership style significantly shaped the direction of one of the world's most influential startup accelerators.

In 2016, Altman expanded his role by becoming president of YC Group, which encompassed three distinct entities:

YC
Core Accelerator Program
YC Continuity
Growth-stage Equity Fund
YC Research
Non-profit Research Lab

Under his guidance, Y Combinator evolved from a small startup accelerator into a comprehensive ecosystem supporting founders at every stage of their entrepreneurial journey.

Expansion of YC

Altman had ambitious goals for Y Combinator's growth, aiming to fund 1,000 new companies per year. This represented a dramatic scaling of YC's operations and influence in the startup ecosystem.

During his tenure, the number of startups funded per batch increased significantly, allowing YC to support more founders and ideas than ever before. This expansion strategy helped cement Y Combinator's position as the world's premier startup accelerator.

New Programs

Altman was a driving force behind several innovative programs that expanded Y Combinator's impact and reach:

2015

YC Fellowship

A program designed to provide early-stage funding to promising startups, with a focus on accessibility and inclusivity.

2015

YC Continuity

A growth-stage investment fund that allowed YC to continue supporting its most promising companies as they scaled.

2015

YC Research

A non-profit research lab focused on developing breakthrough technologies and advancing scientific discovery.

2016

Startup School

A free online program that democratized access to startup knowledge and mentorship.

These initiatives reflected Altman's commitment to expanding Y Combinator's mission beyond its traditional accelerator model, creating new pathways for founders and researchers to access resources and support.

Founder Focus

A hallmark of Altman's leadership was his unwavering commitment to helping founders succeed, even when it came at the expense of fund profits. He believed that by prioritizing founders' needs and success, Y Combinator would ultimately thrive as well.
